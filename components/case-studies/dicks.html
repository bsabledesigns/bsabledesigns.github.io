The Company

Dicks is a comprehensive global provider of cloud-based Human Capital Management (HCM) solutions that unite HR, payroll, talent, time, tax and benefits administration, and a leader in business outsourcing services, analytics and compliance expertise. My team operates as an internal agency within Dicks, redesigning systems to optimize both internal and external user experience with products.

THE PROJECT

As a a part of the Customer Returns team, I was tasked with designing an optimized in-store and online returns process, that would increase both customer and employee satisifaction while generating a cost savings for the company.

THE CHALLENGE

Current return processes were inefficient and overly complicated leading to lower customer satisfaction and reducing repeat customers. Our goal was to create a streamlined process, and the supporting digital elements and infrastructure, that would simplify in-store and online returns. The key challenge in revamping was balancing the needs of both the customer and stakeholder while reducing costs. Working collaboratively as a part of a interdisciplinary team, my role in was to understand and represent the diverse users needs in all phases of the project--research, planning, design and execution--while remaining focused on the business goals of the stakeholder.

My Role

UX, Research & Product Designer

Discovery

The first step in the Discovery phase was to identify and translate the stakeholder's internal goals and business needs into clearly defined, measurable objectives. Once we were aligned on the stakeholderâ€™s objectives, we began researching the as-is returns process to identify weakness, gaps and areas for improvement across the business chain. We interviewed customers, followed employees and customers in-store, read CSAT comments, and observed interactions and processes in customer service and distribution centers. Based on an analysis of the information collected, we were able to identify a number of pain points and opportunities in the current returns process. Among other insights, we identified numerous inefficiencies in the by-mail returns process that were lowering customer satisfaction and losing revenue for the company.

Framing

Our goal in framing was to define a solution that met the needs of the user and to prioritize its key features. Based on our Discovery, we identified many pain points surrounding the mail-in returns process. Once aligned on the core issues the new product should address, we researched best practices and ran ideation sessions where we developed and analyzed potential solutions. Next, we prioritized potential solutions based on their technical complexity and whether they met our objectives, aligned with business needs, and resolved user pains. I led sketching workshops to develop a prototype of the product that met our core objectives and the prioritized business and user needs. Through these exercises, we were able to define a self-service return solution with downloadable shipping labels as our focus.

Validating

A key element of our design process was the validation of solutions. During this phase, we wanted to ensure that all proposed solutions were necessary, met our objectives and added value. One way we did this was by running brief but important experiments. For instance, based on our analysis, we believed that users wanted to be able to download return labels, a hypothesis we were able to validate through running experiments. This led us to focus on developing that particular capability. We repeated the validation process for several other features.

Iterate

Our team focused on using lean processes to build out new self-service capabilities. Through an iterative process involving team sketch sessions and the constant integration of user feedback, I was able to design and build out new self-service returns options for customers. I worked closely with the developers in order to ensure we were always making the best decisions design-wise and technically.

SOLUTION

As an interdisciplinary team, we were able to create a solution that improved the returns process for the user, employees and the business as a whole. My role was not only to advocate for the user, but to empower other team members to be a part of UX process, integrating user needs into all aspects of the design and execution of the solution. With a user-focused approach, we were able to develop a new returns process that increased customer satisfaction and reduced inefficiencies while saving the business over a million dollars a year.
