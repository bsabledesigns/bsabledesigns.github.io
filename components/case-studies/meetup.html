Meetup

The Company

Meetup is a social events app used by over 26 million people. Meetup’s web and mobile app goals are to make it easy for anyone to organize or find local groups.

The challenge

Meetup’s mobile app sign-up process takes over 11+ steps. This will cause Meetup to lose potential users along the way. Users that I interviewed expressed frustration with the sign-up process, with some too frustrated to complete it.

My Role

Research & UX Design

Getting to a solution

User research and user personas assisted in redesigning the sign-up process to take 7 simple steps. During user testing, I noticed that users were frustrated and sometimes discouraged to complete the process. My solution was to move the e-mail and password fields to the beginning of the sign-up process to allow users to skip screens that are optional and start using the app quickly. I also added ability to filter through interests or skip it all together. I took out screens that searched for meetups and groups, which delayed the process and is already provided in the app.

Research

I watched five users as they went through the sign-up process to figure out where the pain points are, and interviewed them after they completed it. I used their feedback to create an easier sign-up process.

User personas

Prototyping

I used InVision to build a prototype, and reiterated on the prototype as problems were found through user testing.
